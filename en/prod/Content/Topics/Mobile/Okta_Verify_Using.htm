Okta Verify

Okta Verify supports multifactor authentication, and is one of the SSOAn acronym for single sign-on. In a SSO system, a user logs in once to the system and can access multiple systems without being prompted to sign in for each one. Okta is a cloud-based SSO platform that allows users to enter one name and password to access multiple applications. Users can access all of their web applications, both behind the firewall and in the cloud, with a single sign in. Okta provides a seamless experience across PCs, laptops, tablets, and smartphones. options that can be used with the Okta service. The numbers are generated using the industry standard Time-Based, One-Time Password Algorithm (TOTP). Okta Verify is available for iPhone, Android and Windows devices.

Okta Verify includes several enhanced features, including the following:

Okta Verify with Push Authentication

Push notifications enable users to verify their identity with a single tap on their mobile device—without the need to type a code. This gets them directly into their apps while retaining the same level of security. This feature is available for iPhone, Android and Windows devices.

For more information about using Okta Verify Multifactor Authentication (MFA), see Multifactor Authentication.

The following provides details on enabling this feature, as well as the end user experience.

End User Experience After Enablement

End users experience the following on their next sign in to Okta.

After successfully signing into Okta, the Okta Verify Authentication challenge screen appears.

Ensure you have access to your mobile device as you are prompted to verify by either:

  • Selecting Send Push to use push authentication. OR
  • Tapping the Okta Verify app on your mobile device and entering the one-time code into the Enter code field. If you opted to configure Okta Verify manually, you will only have the option to enter a code, not to push to your device.

If you opted for push authentication, you have two options:

  • Have a notification sent out to your device:

    Tap the Approve icon on your device to approve the verification request.

    You are signed into your account without any further action.

  • Use the automatic push option:

    Click the Send push automatically check-box.

    For this first instance, you are still required to click the Send Push button on the Okta Sign On page. After you've completed this initial verification, simply touching the Approve button from your mobile device signs you into Okta without further action.

Upgrading to Okta Verify with Push

If you want to prompt your users to upgrade to a version of Okta Verify that supports Push Authentication, first enable that functionality. For details see Admin Configuration, above. When enabled, the next time your end users use Okta Verify, they will see a Please update your profile screen with a button allowing them to immediately upgrade. Your end users can either upgrade or click Remind me later to continue without upgrading. If they chose to be reminded, a prompt will be shown the next time they sign in.

Okta Verify with Touch ID

Touch ID uses Touch ID technology to guard against unauthorized use of Okta Verify. Admins can configure an end-user fingerprint request, which appears after the initial MFA challenge. If the user's device is lost or stolen, no one else can gain access to it. This feature is currently only available for iOS devices.

The following assumes that you have enabled Okta Verify for your org. If not, see Admin Configuration for information on this initial configuration.

Admin Configuration for Okta Verify with Touch ID

Once Touch ID is enabled, your end users will be prompted to configure Touch ID for their device during enrollment or authentication challenge. The device UI displays instructions to help users through this configuration process, as described in End User Configuration for Okta Verify.

To enable this feature, do the following:

  1. Log into Okta as an administrator.

  2. From the Okta Dashboard, navigate to Security Authentication Multifactor tab.

  3. Under Factor Types, click the Edit button.

  4. Under Okta Verify, check the Require TouchID for Okta Verify checkbox, as shown below.

  5. Click the Save button.

Note: Enabling Require TouchID for Okta Verify does not prevent end users with devices that do not support TouchID from using Okta Verify to authenticate into your org.

End User Configuration for Okta Verify with Touch ID

Your end users first need to upgrade to the latest version of the Okta Verify app (Version 2.5.2 for iOS).

If your end-users are already enrolled in Okta Verify with Push, and you simply enable Touch ID for your org, there is very little setup required for your users. The next time they authenticate with Push, the response depends on whether their fingerprint has been captured by the native iOS device.

  • If their fingerprint has not been captured by the iOS device, clicking the Send Push button on the Okta Verify authentication page activates the Touch ID Required screen on their device (as shown in Step 1 below).

Using Multiple Accounts

When multiple accounts exist, Touch ID accounts are distinct from the other authentication options in several ways.

Icons

Touch ID accounts are identified by their thumbprint icon and obfuscated numbers. End users touch the fingerprint icon to expose the authentication number.

These accounts are also distinct when Touch ID is required by an org adminAn abbreviation of administrator. This is the individual(s) who have access to the Okta Administrator Dashboard. They control the provisioning and deprovisioning of end users, the assigning of apps, the resetting of passwords, and the overall end user experience. Only administrators have the Administration button on the upper right side of the My Applications page.. If an admin requires Touch ID authentication and it has not yet been setup by an end user, alert icons appear on each account. The user touches an alert icon, then the Touch ID Setup Required message appears with instructions for Touch ID setup in device Settings.

Grouped authentication

If more than one Touch ID account is being used, verifying one account will simultaneously expose them all—every authentication number is visible. They remain for a one-minute grace period, then return to an obfuscated state.

Admin Configuration

To enable Okta Verify, do the following:

  1. Log into Okta as an administrator.

  2. Navigate to Security > Multifactor page.

  3. Click the Factor Type tab.
  4. Click the Edit button.
  5. Under Acceptable factors, check Okta Verify. See above for information on enabling Push Notification and Touch ID.
  6. Click the Save button.

The previous steps allow for an org-level update. If you wish to enable Okta Verify with Push on a per-group level, see Multifactor Policies.

End User Configuration

The first time end users sign into their orgs after you configure this factor, they see the Extra verification is required for your account page. To being the setup process, they must do the following:

  1. Click the Setup button.
  2. From your mobile device, follow the instructions to download and install the Okta Verify app, then click Next.
  3. Configure Okta Verify to link it to your Okta account. You can scan a QR code or manually enter a code.

Setup using a QR Code

  1. On your phone, start the Okta Verify app, tap the Add Account button on iOS, or the + button on Android.
  2. Scan the QR code on your computer screen using your device camera.

The pass code generator screen appears and generates pass codes to use when prompted for extra verification. You have 30 seconds to enter the pass code before it generates a new one.

Manual Configuration

You can also configure an account manually by doing the following:

  1. On your mobile device, start the Okta Verify app, then tap the Add Account button on iOS, or the + button on Android.
  2. From the barcode page, tap the No barcode? button.
  3. In the Okta Account field, enter your Okta username (for example, ted@mycompany.com).
  4. From your computer, click the Can’t scan the QR code link so that you can get the secret key and enter it in the Secret Key field on your mobile device.
  5. Tap the Save button.

The pass code generator screen appears and generates the codes to use when prompted for extra verification. You have 30 seconds to enter the pass code before it generates a new one.

Using Multiple Accounts

End users can use Okta Verify for multiple accounts, and even organizations outside of Okta.

Adding an Account

To add a new account, users simply tap the Add Account button on iOS, or the + button on Android. When multiple accounts exist, they are displayed in the order through which they were enrolled — the oldest on top.

Reordering Accounts

To reorder existing accounts:

  • iOS: Tap the Edit button to access edit mode. Then, hold the gripper icon on the right-side of the tile and drag to the preferred location.
  • Android: Tap the three dots to the right of the account that you want to move. Tap Reorder, then hold the gripper icon on the right-side of the tile and drag to the preferred location.

Editing an Account Name

To edit an account name:

  • iOS: First tap the Edit button to the top-right corner to enter edit mode, then tap the organization name you wish to change.
  • Android: Tap the three dots icon on the right side of the account you want to edit, then tap Rename.

Deleting an Account

To delete an existing account:

  • iOS: Tap the Edit button to access the edit view, then tap the red delete icon to remove the account from the list.
  • Android: Tap the three dots to the right of the account you want to delete, then tap Delete.

On both iOS and Android, end users are asked to confirm that they want to delete the account before the action is completed. Deletion of an account is permanent and prevents sign on for the account.

Apple Watch and Android Wear

Okta Verify is also available as an Apple Watch or Android Wear app. Your end users can search for and install Okta Verify as they would any other app. If Okta Verify is configured as an authentication factor for your org, your end users can easily link Okta Verify to their Okta account, as described in Configuring Multifactor Authentication.

Apple Watch

The Okta Verify for Apple Watch app allows you to view and accept or deny Okta Verify with Push challenges from your watch screen. The watch also displays a rotating one-time password to allow authentication if Push is not enabled, or if internet connectivity is unavailable.

Once you've paired your watch with an iPhone, the Okta Verify app installs automatically. Note that while the app can be opened and used at any time, the watch will only receive notifications from the iPhone if it is locked. This is per Apple design.

Android Wear

The Okta Verify for Android Wear app allows you to view and accept or deny Okta Verify with Push challenges from your watch screen. End users can also access the OTP on their Android Wear devices.

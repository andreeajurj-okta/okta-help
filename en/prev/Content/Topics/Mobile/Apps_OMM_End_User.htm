Okta Mobility Management End-User Setup

Overview

The Okta Mobility Management (OMM) program secures your mobile device and configures it to access your work email, calendar, contacts and applications. As part of the enrollment you are required to set a pin or password on your mobile device. The Okta policy allows for a 4-digit pin.

Your privacy is very important to Okta. Corporate data is protected while your personal data remains separate and not accessible to your organization. We only manage information required to secure and protect corporate data.

The following list shows what we can and cannot manage:

What OMMAn acronym for Okta Mobility Management. OMM enables you to manage your users' mobile devices, applications, and data. Your users enroll in the service and can then download and use managed apps from the Apps Store. Managed apps are typically work-related, such as Box or Expensify. As an administrator, you can remove managed apps and associated data from users' devices at any time. You can configure policies, such as data sharing controls, on any of your managed apps. See Configuring Okta Mobility Management for more information. Can See What OMM Cannot See
Device Name Personal Mail, Calendars, and Contacts
*Phone Number SMS or Text Messages
Serial Number Browser History
Model Name and Number FaceTime or Phone Call Logs
Capacity and Space Available Personal Reminders and Notes
OS Version Number Frequency of All Use
**Names of Installed Apps Device Location

*As derived from company directories, not from the phone.

**For personal apps, OMM can only see the names, none of the content of the apps.

The Okta Mobility management program has permission to deprovision only your work accounts and applications, which requires a special request with IT. You may factory reset your device erasing all of its contents if it is stolen. Note that your IT department can also factory reset your device.

The following procedures assume that you are currently an Okta user and assigned to a group that includes a mobile policy.

Setting Up Okta Mobility Management on your iOS Devices
  1. Install the Okta Mobile application on your iOS device.
  2. Tap the Okta Mobile appAn abbreviation of application. Essentially, it is a web-based site used to perform any number of specific tasks, and requires authentication from end users by signing in., then sign in to Okta Mobile:

    Note: For Oktapreview tenants, please enter the entire URL in the Site name field (for example: https://yourcompany.oktapreview.com).


  3. Set up and confirm a new Okta Mobile PIN:

    Okta recognizes that the device and OrgAn abbreviation of organization, but can also be thought of as a company. A company that uses Okta as their SSO portal is generally referred to as an org. As an administrator, you decide how Okta should be displayed and/or integrated with your org. are both registered and eligible for Okta Mobility Management (OMM) and automatically prompts you for enrollment.

  4. If your organization has set up two-factor authentication, you will need to verify your account using Okta Verify or by sending a code to your phone.

  5. Then you will be prompted to secure your device in order to access work resources.

  6. Install the Okta OTA Device Attribute Request.

  7. The installation process begins. During the installation, the following iOS screens/prompts appear:

    Note: During the installation process, you may be prompted for your device's pin.

    • The Install Profile screen. Select Install to continue.

    • A Warning screen. Select Install to continue.

    • A Remote Management popup dialog asks if you trust this profile's source. Select Trust:


  8. Once the installation is complete, you may be redirected to safari and have to select Open to Open Okta Mobile.

  9. The device is now enrolled in OMM and the Okta Apps Store is displayed.

    If you have been assigned any mobile-enabled applications, you will immediately see those apps listed in the Okta Apps Store. You still need to install each application.


  10. Install any app on your device by selecting INSTALL. Once an app is installed, the INSTALL button turns into an OPEN button. Select OPEN to configure the native app for this device.

Note: If any of the apps already exist on the device, they are considered unmanaged and are not subject to Okta's security policies, they will however, show up on the Okta Apps store page. You should remove any unmanaged versions of managed apps from your device, then reopen the Okta mobile app.

Note: If the Managed Open-In Policy is set to Deny access for managed apps to open in unmanaged apps, documents in managed apps can only be opened by other managed apps installed on that device.

Setting Up Okta Mobility Management on your Android Devices
  1. Install the Okta Mobile application on your Android device.
  2. Tap the Okta Mobile app, then sign in to Okta Mobile:

    Note: For Oktapreview tenants, please enter the entire URL in the Site name field (for example: https://yourcompany.oktapreview.com).


  3. Set up and confirm a new PIN. Select Done when complete.

  4. You will then be prompted to secure your device in order to access work resources. Select Get Started > Secure now to begin setting up your work profile.

    Note: During the set up process you may be prompted for your device's pin.



  5. The next steps differ depending on what kind of enrollment you are using:
Android for Work Enrollment
  1. The following Google screens appear to take you through the process of setting up your work profile:
    • A Set up work profile screen. Select Next.

    • A dialog describing the abilities and permissions of your adminAn abbreviation of administrator. This is the individual(s) who have access to the Okta Administrator Dashboard. They control the provisioning and deprovisioning of end users, the assigning of apps, the resetting of passwords, and the overall end user experience. Only administrators have the Administration button on the upper right side of the My Applications page.. Select OK to continue setting up your work profile.

      Note: If your device does not have a passcode, you may be prompted to set one.

      Note: If you have not encrypted your device, you may be prompted to do so. This may take an hour or more, so connect your device to a power source and make sure you have time to complete the process.

  2. After your work profile has been set up, you are taken back to the Okta app and prompted to enter your PIN. Use the Okta Mobile PIN you created.

  3. If your organization does not use GSuite, skip to step 6. If it does, a Google screen appears to take you to your org's sign in page, and asks you to accept the Privacy Policy and Terms of Service. Select Accept to continue.

  4. You are prompted to sign in to your org account. Enter your organization credentials, then select Next to continue.

  5. A Google screen appears describing the details of your new account and how it is administrated. Select Accept to agree to the Google terms of service and continue.

  6. Your device has been successfully enrolled in OMM, and Play for Work starts to enable. The process may take a few minutes. If your device locks during the wait, you may have to re-enter your Okta Mobile PIN. You can then access Play for Work by selecting the Menu icon in the top left corner, then selecting Play for Work in the sidebar that appears.

  7. After Play for Work has been enabled, a terms of service dialog appears. Select Accept.

  8. The Play for Work app store is displayed, with all the mobile-enabled applications you have been assigned.

  9. Select any app to go to its detailed page, then select Install to install it on your device. Once an app is installed, the Install button turns into an Open button. Select Open to configure the native app for this device.

Samsung SAFE Enrollment

If you are using a Samsung device that does not support Android for Work, follow these steps.

  1. The Samsung Knox privacy policy appears. Select Accept to continue.
  2. An Activate device administrator? dialog describing the permissions of your device administrator appears. Select Activate to continue.
  3. You are taken to the Mobile Apps Store, where you will see any apps your administrator has assigned to you.

  4. Select Install to be taken to the app's page on the Google Play Store, then select Install to install it on your device. Once an app is installed, the Install button turns into an Open button.

Native Android Enrollment

If you are using a non-Samsung device that does not support Android for Work, follow these steps.

  1. A dialog appears asking you to enroll in OMM for access to company resources. Select Enroll to continue.
  2. An Activate device administrator? dialog describing the permissions of your device administrator appears .Select Activate to continue.
  3. You are taken to the Mobile Apps Store, where you will see any apps your administrator has assigned to you.

  4. Select Install to be taken to the app's page on the Google Play Store, then select Install to install it on your device. Once an app is installed, the Install button turns into an Open button.

Note: If any of the apps already exist on the device, they are considered unmanaged and are not subject to Okta's security policies, they will however, show up on the Okta Apps store page. You should to remove any unmanaged versions of managed apps from your device, then reopen the Okta mobile app.

Note: If the Managed Open-In Policy is set to Deny access for managed apps to open in unmanaged apps, documents in managed apps can only be opened by other managed apps installed on that device.

Adding New Apps to your Device

When a new app is made available for you, that app automatically appears in the Mobile Apps Store on your device. Click INSTALL to install.

Note that you can uninstall a managed app at any time. Okta Mobility Management does not prevent them from doing so.

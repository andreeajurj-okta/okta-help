Enforce Okta Device Trust for Native mobile apps and Chrome on MDM-managed Android devices (Beta)

Okta Device Trust for AirWatch by VMware and MobileIron Cloud-managed Android devices allows you to prevent unmanaged Android devices from accessing corporate SAMLAn acronym for Security Assertion Markup Language, SAML is an XML-based standard for exchanging authentication and authorization data between an identity provider (IdP) and a service provider (SP). The SAML standard addresses issues unique to the single sign-on (SSO) solution, and defines three roles: the end user, the IDP, and the SP. and WS-Fed cloud apps. It works with Okta Mobile for Android to provide a frictionless end user experience.

  • Android devices running Android 5.0+
  • Okta Mobile for Android 3.8.0+ is installed
  • AirWatch by VMware or MobileIron Cloud MDM solution
  • Necessary only during the Beta and Early Access phases of this Device Trust solution: Make sure that Okta Customer Support has enabled this Device Trust solution for your orgThe Okta container that represents a real-world organization.. Part Ⓔ — In Okta: Configure app Sign On policy rules


Click: Important to know before you begin
  • Only AppAn abbreviation of application. Essentially, it is a web-based site used to perform any number of specific tasks, and requires authentication from end users by signing in., Org, and Super admins can configure app Sign On policies.
  • When processing a Sign On policy rule for an app configured with the Trust or Not trusted condition, Okta suspends normal rule processing and lets Okta Mobile assess whether or not the device is trusted. If Okta Mobile assesses the device to be untrusted, one of the following occurs:
    • If you configured a Sign On policy rule to deny untrusted devices, users are prompted to enroll in the MDM provider configured in Security > Device Trust.
    • If you configured a Sign On policy rule to issue an MFA challenge to users with untrusted devices, such users are challenged with MFA.

  • Do not disable the Android Device Trust setting on the Security > Device Trust page if you have also configured an app sign on policy that allows trusted Android devices. Otherwise, your Device Trust configuration will be in an inconsistent state and users with untrusted devices may not be shown messages with guidance on how to secure their device.

  • (Applies only during the Early Access phase of this Device Trust solution) If you ask Okta Support to disable this Device Trust solution for your org (which is separate from the global Device Trust setting that you enabled in Part A), make sure to first change the Device Trust setting in the app sign on policy rules to Any (Applications > app > Sign On). If you do not make this change and then later have Okta Support re-enable Device Trust capability for your org, the Device Trust setting in app sign on policy rules will take effect immediately, which you may not have expected.

Caveats
  • End users are not redirected to the app sign-in page under some circumstances – When this Device Trust solution is enabled, if end users sign in to an Okta-federated Gmail or Salesforce account and later delete the account, they are redirected to the Okta Home page instead of the app's sign out page.
  • Securing an Okta-federated MDM application with this Device Trust solution — Okta recommends that you not apply a Not Trusted - Deny app sign policy to your Okta-federated MDM application. Doing so will prevent new users from enrolling their device in your MDM application and accessing other device trust-secured apps.
  • Timeout issue can cause an SSOAn acronym for single sign-on. In a SSO system, a user logs in once to the system and can access multiple systems without being prompted to sign in for each one. Okta is a cloud-based SSO platform that allows users to enter one name and password to access multiple applications. Users can access all of their web applications, both behind the firewall and in the cloud, with a single sign in. Okta provides a seamless experience across PCs, laptops, tablets, and smartphones. error — End users signing in to device trust-secured apps from an untrusted Android device are prompted to enroll their device with their org's MDM vendor. (This is expected behavior.) But if the secured apps are Box, Dropbox, or Salesforce and MDM enrollment takes longer than 10 minutes, or if the end user waits longer than 10 minutes after enrollment before trying again to access the app, an SSO error occurs because the app session has timed out. Advise affected end users to try to access the app again.
  • Devices unenrolled from the MDM still show as enrolled — After unenrolling AfW+MDM-enrolled Android devices from the MDM using the Settings app on the device, such devices may continue to be reported in the MDM API as MDM-enrolled. To deprovision devices from the MDM in this case, remove the associated profile from the MDM admin portal.
  • Gmail screen may display repeatedly during authentication flow — When adding a Device Trust or MFA-secured GSuite account to their personal Gmail account, end users are presented Gmail's Add an email address screen several times during the authentication flow, which may cause confusion. Despite the repeated screen, the state of the auth flow is retained.

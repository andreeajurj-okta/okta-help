The Private App Store

A private appAn abbreviation of application. Essentially, it is a web-based site used to perform any number of specific tasks, and requires authentication from end users by signing in. is a native app (an internally developed enterprise app) created by a customer who wants to distribute it to their end-usersIn Okta literature, we generally refer to "users" as the people who serve as Okta administrators. When we refer to "end users" we are generally referring to the people who the administrators serve. I.e., those who use Okta chiclets to access their apps, but have no administrative control.. The Private App store allows admins to upload these apps to Okta, then distribute them via Okta Mobility Management (OMMAn acronym for Okta Mobility Management. OMM enables you to manage your users' mobile devices, applications, and data. Your users enroll in the service and can then download and use managed apps from the Apps Store. Managed apps are typically work-related, such as Box or Expensify. As an administrator, you can remove managed apps and associated data from users' devices at any time. You can configure policies, such as data sharing controls, on any of your managed apps. See Configuring Okta Mobility Management for more information.). If your app supports managed app configuration, see Managed Application Configurations.

Note: Private apps are available in the Mobile App Store, but they do not appear on the end user‘s Okta Home page.

Before You Begin

For Android apps, make sure that your app has been published in Google Play for Work. For details, see https://support.google.com/googleplay/work/answer/6145139?hl=en.

Upload Your Native App

  1. From the adminAn abbreviation of administrator. This is the individual(s) who have access to the Okta Administrator Dashboard. They control the provisioning and deprovisioning of end users, the assigning of apps, the resetting of passwords, and the overall end user experience. Only administrators have the Administration button (shown below) on the upper right side of the My Applications page. Dashboard, go to Applications > Applications > Add Application, then click Create New App.

  2. Select Native app from the Platform drop-down menu.

  3. Select a Sign on method.

  4. Click Create

  5. Configure the app according to your selected Sign on method:

    If you selected None (no credentials or sign on method associated with the app):

    1. Click Create.

    2. Under General Settings, enter a Name for the app, then click Finish.

    If you selected SAMLAn acronym for Security Assertion Markup Language, SAML is an XML-based standard for exchanging authentication and authorization data between an identity provider (IdP) and a service provider (SP). The SAML standard addresses issues unique to the single sign-on (SSO) solution, and defines three roles: the end user, the IDP, and the SP. 2.0:

    1. Under General Settings, enter a Name for the app, then click Next.

    2. Configure your SAML authentication settings, then click Finish.

    If you selected OpenID Connect:

    1. Under General Settings, enter an Application Name for the app, then click Next.

    2. Under Configure OpenID Connect, add a Redirect UI, then click Finish.

  6. From the Add Native App drop-down menu, select the type of app (iOS or Android) that you plan to upload.


    The Upload Mobile App page appears.

  7. Upload the app. Note the size limitation of 4GB for private apps.

    For an iOS App

    Browse to the App binary file, enter any other option settings, then click Save.

    For an Android App

    1. Enter the App Package ID, then click Configure App.

    2. Configure any other settings including Permissions (for details, see About Run Time Permissions below), then click Save.

Your app now appears within the Native Application list under the Mobile tab, and is available in the Mobile App Store. Once deployed, it's available to your end usersIn Okta literature, we refer to "end users" as the people who have their own Okta home page (My Applications), using chiclets to authenticate into all of their apps. End users do not have any administrative control. When we refer to "users" we are generally referring to the individual(s) who have administrative control..

Note: Private apps are available in the Mobile Apps Store, but they do not appear on the end users' Okta Home page.

Other settings available for mobile apps are described in Enabling Access for Mobile Applications.

Private App Updates

  1. From the Okta Dashboard, click to the Applications drop-down menu.
  2. From the Applications page, navigate to your private app.
  3. Click the Mobile tab.
  4. Click the Edit button
  5. From the Where is the app located drop-down menu, choose Update app to new version.
  6. Click Browse to find your .ipa file. Keep the following in mind before uploading.

Your app must:

  • Have a matching bundle ID. If you upload an iPhone, iPad, or universal app with a different bundle ID, you must delete the previously existing .ipa to upload a new one.
  • Have a valid provisioning profile.
  • Be compatible with your device. Uploading one universal app replaces the need for separate iPad and iPhone .ipa files. You can instead, upload one version each type.
  • Have a higher version number than the existing version of the app.
  • Use version numbers with integers only. Use of any alpha characters will result in an error. Example: Version 2.1.1.

Once uploaded, your end users can update the app from the Mobile App Store in Okta Mobile.

End-User Experience

When an end user goes to the Okta Mobile App Store, a section titled Update displays all apps with available updates.

  1. View the Update section.
  2. Tap the Update button of the app.
  3. Once prompted, select Install. After the updated app installs, your app icon moves from the Update section to the Installed Applications page.

App Security for iOS

Apps installed through the Okta Mobile App Store are automatically secured through OMM. The apps remain secured as long as the device is enrolled in OMM.

To secure an app installed outside of the Okta Mobile App Store:

  1. Search for your app within the Mobile App Store.
  2. Select Secure.

If you uninstall the app, then re-install it through Okta Mobile Store, the app remains secured. You will only see the option to secure the app again if you remove OMM enrollment, then subsequently re-enroll.

To learn more go to Enabling Mobile Access to Applications.
